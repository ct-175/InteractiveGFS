Early Settlements

Humans have lived in Britain for thousands of years. Key periods:

  • Stone Age – first tools and hunter-gatherers
  • Bronze Age – metal tools and early farming
  • Iron Age – hill forts and tribal societies

Fun Fact: Stonehenge was built during the late Neolithic period!

The Roman Era

The Romans invaded Britain in 43 AD and introduced roads, baths, and Hadrian's Wall.

London was founded by the Romans and called Londinium.

The Middle Ages

Key events:

  • Norman Conquest – 1066
  • Magna Carta signed – 1215
  • Hundred Years' War – 1337–1453
  • Black Death – mid-14th century

The Tudor Period

Famous monarchs:

  • Henry VIII – established the Church of England
  • Elizabeth I – defeated the Spanish Armada in 1588

The Industrial Revolution

18th–19th century: Britain becomes a global industrial power.

  • Steam engines and mechanized factories
  • Urbanization and social change
  • Innovations in transport and communication

The 20th Century

Major events:

  • World War I (1914–1918) & II (1939–1945)
  • Formation of the NHS – 1948
  • Decolonization of the British Empire

Modern UK

The UK today:

  • Constitutional monarchy and parliamentary democracy
  • Prime Minister is head of government
  • Member of the Commonwealth
  • Major financial and cultural influence worldwide
